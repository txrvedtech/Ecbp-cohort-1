# Week 1 – Evaluation System Framework

## Task 1 – Capability Factors

- Structured Thinking Under Ambiguity
   - Ability to create a clear approach even when the problem is unclear or incomplete. 
- Assumption Identification & Validation
   - Ability to explicitly state assumptions and check if they are valid instead of blindly proceeding. 
- Trade-off Awareness in Decision-Making
   - Ability to recognize compromises (time vs quality, speed vs accuracy) and choose consciously. 
- Depth of Problem Exploration
   - Ability to go beyond surface-level understanding and identify root causes, not just symptoms. 
- Iterative Improvement Mindset
   - Ability to refine solutions continuously based on feedback, errors, or new information.


## Task 2 – Observation Methods

- Structured Thinking Under Ambiguity  
  - Give a problem with incomplete/unclear information  
  - Observe how candidate defines the problem  
  - Check if they create a structured approach before solving  

- Assumption Identification & Validation  
  - Present a scenario with hidden assumptions  
  - Observe if candidate explicitly states assumptions  
  - Check if they validate or question them  

- Trade-off Awareness in Decision-Making  
  - Provide multiple solution options with pros/cons  
  - Observe how candidate compares choices  
  - Check if decision is justified using trade-offs  

- Depth of Problem Exploration  
  - Give a problem with an obvious surface-level answer  
  - Observe if candidate asks deeper “why” questions  
  - Check if they identify root causes instead of jumping to conclusions  

- Iterative Improvement Mindset  
  - Introduce feedback or flaws after initial solution  
  - Observe how candidate responds  
  - Check if they refine or improve their approach
