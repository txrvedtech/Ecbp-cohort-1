Task 1: Capability Factors

Problem Framing Ability  
- Understands the problem before starting  
- Identifies what is given and what is missing  

Structured Thinking & Communication  
- Thinks step by step  
- Explains ideas clearly  

Adaptability & Learning Ability  
- Changes approach when needed  
- Learns from mistakes  

Attention to Edge Cases  
- Thinks about what might go wrong  
- Does not focus only on normal cases  

Ownership & Execution Discipline  
- Completes work properly  
- Takes responsibility  

---

Task 2: Observation Method

Problem Framing Ability  
- Asks questions before starting  
- Explains problem in own words  
- Identifies unclear parts  

Structured Thinking & Communication  
- Explains step by step  
- Keeps it simple  
- Maintains logical flow  

Adaptability & Learning Ability  
- Changes method when stuck  
- Accepts feedback  
- Avoids repeating mistakes  

Attention to Edge Cases  
- Mentions failure scenarios  
- Checks different conditions  
- Does not assume ideal case  

Ownership & Execution Discipline  
- Completes tasks without reminders  
- Shows consistency  
- Takes responsibility for mistakes  

---

Task 3: Failure Conditions

- Starts without understanding the problem  
- Continues same approach even when it fails  
- Ignores edge cases  

---

Task 4: Evaluation Process

- Give a problem  
- Ask candidate to explain approach  
- Ask questions during explanation  
- Observe thinking and responses  
- Check how they handle confusion  
- Identify strengths and improvements
